The Heron framework, developed by John Heron, provides a model for analyzing how help is delivered. It encompasses two basic styles: “authoritative” and “facilitative”, which further break down into six categories describing how people intervene when providing assistance1. Here’s a concise summary of the framework:

  1. Authoritative Style:

    • Prescriptive: Clear instructions given to employees, suitable for time-sensitive situations or inexperienced staff.
    • Informative: Sharing personal experience to aid less experienced team members.
    • Confronting: Questioning employees’ approaches to encourage independent thinking while still overseeing them.

  2. Facilitative Style:

    • Cathartic: Encouraging expression of emotions and frustrations to identify issues.
    • Catalytic: Aiding reflection on individual abilities2.
