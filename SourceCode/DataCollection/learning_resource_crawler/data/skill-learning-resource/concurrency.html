Concurrency in computer science refers to the ability of different parts or units of a program, algorithm, or problem to be executed out-of-order or in partial order, without affecting the outcome. It allows for parallel execution of these concurrent units, which can significantly improve overall speed in multi-processor and multi-core systems1.
