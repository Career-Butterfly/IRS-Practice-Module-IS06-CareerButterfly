NoSQL, short for “not only SQL,” refers to non-relational databases that use a flexible schema model to store data in formats like documents, key-value pairs, wide columns, and graphs, providing scalability and ease of development1. Here are five free resources to learn more about NoSQL databases:

  1. Google Cloud’s NoSQL Databases: Explore Google Cloud’s offerings like Bigtable, Memorystore, and Firestore.
  2. DATAVERSITY’s NoSQL Tutorial: Understand the basics of NoSQL databases and their features.
  3. IBM’s NoSQL Database Overview: Learn about NoSQL database design and querying outside traditional relational structures.
  4. MongoDB’s NoSQL Explained: Dive into the features, types, and benefits of NoSQL databases, including MongoDB.
  5. Codecademy’s Introduction to NoSQL: Get started with NoSQL and explore different types of NoSQL databases.

Feel free to explore these resources to enhance your understanding of NoSQL! 🚀
